The gaming industry has evolved rapidly. THe first game ever to be put into an arcade was pong, a game where a white dot bounces back and forth across the screen. The player is a white paddle represented as a white pixelated line. The goal was to score by hitting the ball past your opponent. Now we have reached the era of virtual reality and people endlessly testing Unity Engine to maximize its performance. I see clips every week showing side by side comparisons of places in real life, rendered in Unreal Unity Engine and you can barely tell the difference. One day we'll get games looking like films we watch in theaters at our finger tips.
